Proverbs are the soul of a culture - carrying generations of wisdom in a single breath.

Good intention is half the worship

نیت نیک، نصف عبادت است

Explanation: In Islam and Afghan culture, intention (niyyat) is very important; having a sincere heart is already part of doing good.

A slap makes the face red, not warm

سیلی صورت را سرخ می‌سازد، نه گرم

Explanation: Some actions may look helpful or affectionate but are actually harmful. Don’t confuse pain with care.

A snake-bitten person fears even a black rope

مار گزیده از ریسمان سیاه می‌ترسد

Explanation: When someone has been hurt or traumatized, they become overly cautious, even about harmless things.

You can’t weave someone’s fate into a black rug

گلیم بخت کسی را سیاه نتوان بافت

Explanation: You can’t change someone’s destiny or bring them misfortune by force — fate is beyond control.

The mature recognizes the mature, the raw sees only the raw

پُخته را پُخته شناسد، خام را خام

Explanation: Wise people recognize wisdom in others, while the inexperienced may fail to see it.

Where there’s smoke, there’s fire

هر جا که دود است، آن‌جا آتش است

Explanation: If there’s a sign of trouble, there is probably a real issue behind it.

Spring doesn't come with just one flower

با یک گل بهار نمی‌شود

Explanation: One good thing or person isn’t enough to bring complete change — true transformation needs more effort and participation.

Wherever you go, the sky is the same color

هر کجا که بروی، آسمان همان رنگ است

Explanation: Escaping from problems doesn’t always help — some challenges follow you wherever you are.
